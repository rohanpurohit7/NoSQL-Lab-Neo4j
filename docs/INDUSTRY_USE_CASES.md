# Industry Use Case Narrative

## Knowledge Graphs

Neo4j-style graph modeling is useful when relationships are the center of the question. A graph can connect people, systems, assets, courses, documents, locations, products, or events.

## Financial Services

Graph databases can support relationship-driven analysis such as fraud rings, account relationships, merchant networks, claims relationships, and entity resolution. Results should support investigation rather than automatic conclusions.

## Cybersecurity and IT Operations

Graph models can represent identity relationships, device dependencies, application connections, access paths, and infrastructure ownership. This helps analysts reason about blast radius and dependency chains.

## Retail and Recommendations

Graphs can connect users, products, categories, purchases, reviews, and similarity relationships. This supports recommendation logic and customer journey analysis.

## Public Sector and Healthcare

With approved de-identified data, graph methods can model case relationships, service dependencies, referral networks, or operational workflows. Public examples should use synthetic entities.

## Applied Value

This lab demonstrates nodes, relationships, pattern queries, traversal, and interpretation. The applied lesson is to model the relationship question first, then validate that returned paths mean what the analyst thinks they mean.
